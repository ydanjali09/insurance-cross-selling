 📊 **Insurance Cross-Selling Analysis**
 
 📌**Project Overview**

This project focuses on analyzing customer data to identify potential opportunities for cross-selling insurance products. The goal is to understand customer behavior and determine which segments are more likely to purchase additional insurance policies.
---
 🎯 **Objective**

* Identify high-potential customers for cross-selling
* Analyze factors influencing customer conversion
* Provide business insights to improve marketing strategies

---

🛠️ **Tools & Technologies**
* Python
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn

---
📂 **Dataset Features**

The dataset includes customer-related information such as:

* Age
* Income
* Family Members
* Gender
* Marital Status
* Education
* Occupation
* Job Title
* Current Product & Product Type
* New Product Type
* Customer Rating (Cold, Warm, Hot)
* Coverage Amounts
* Conversion Status (Purchased / Not Purchased)

---
📊 **Analysis Performed**

 1.**Univariate Analysis**
* Distribution of numerical variables (Age, Income, Coverage)
* Count analysis of categorical variables
* Understanding customer demographics

---

 2.**Bivariate Analysis**

* Numerical variables vs Conversion using boxplots
* Categorical variables vs Conversion using countplots
* Identification of patterns affecting purchase decisions

---

 3. **Multivariate Analysis**

* Correlation heatmap to study relationships between variables
* Conversion rate comparison across multiple categories

---

 🔍**Key Insights**

* Customers aged **35–50** show higher conversion rates
* Income range **3–9 lakhs** is more likely to purchase insurance
* Customers with **2–5 family members** are key targets
* **Married individuals** have higher purchase probability
* **Educated customers** are more aware and responsive
* **SPT & SE occupations** show strong conversion trends
* **Hot-rated customers** have the highest likelihood of purchase
* Existing customers are ideal for **cross-selling strategies**

---

 📈**Business Recommendations**
* Focus marketing efforts on high-income, mid-age customers
* Target customers with family responsibilities
* Prioritize hot and warm leads for better conversion
* Promote relevant products to existing customers
* Avoid aggressive selling to low-interest (cold) customers
